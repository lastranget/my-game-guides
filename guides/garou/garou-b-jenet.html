Garou uses four attack buttons: A=weak punch, B=weak kick, C=strong punch, D=strong kick. (No medium attacks.)

Directions are arrows from your character's point of view (facing right): ↓↘→ = roll from down to forward. A move like Power Wave = ↓↘→+ means "quarter-circle forward + a punch". Combos read left→right; › separates steps.

Fighting-game basics (if you're brand new)

  1. Block by holding away. Hold ← away from the opponent for high/standing hits, ↙ (down-back) for lows. You can't block in the air.
  2. Chain your normals. Press weak→strong: a light (/) into a strong (/) cancels automatically, and a strong normal cancels into a special. That's a combo.
  3. Hops & jumps. Tap up briefly for a short hop (fast, great for pressure); hold up for a full jump. Hopping in is safer and a core Garou habit.
  4. Specials cost nothing; supers cost meter. The bar at the bottom fills as you fight; spend a stock on a super (↓↘→↓↘→+) for big damage or chip.
  5. Throw turtles. Walk up, →/←+ close = a throw to crack a blocker.

The four things that make Garou special

  1. Just Defense. Tap ← at the last second as a hit lands (instead of just holding back). You'll see "JUST DEFENDED", recover a sliver of health, and gain almost no blockstun — so you can instantly counter (a Guard Cancel). This is the heart of Garou; even tapping back to block trains the habit.
  2. Feints / "braking". →+ or ↓+ performs only the start of a special, then cancels it. This lets you cancel a normal into "nothing" and keep attacking — the basis of Garou's bigger combos (written as brake below).
  3. Blowback attack. (press C+D together) is a hard knock-away that makes space and can start okizeme. Beginners can lean on it to reset a scramble.
  4. T.O.P. system. Before the match you place a T.O.P. zone on one third of your life bar. While your health sits in that orange zone you flash "T.O.P. IN!" and get: a unique T.O.P. attack (), more damage, and slow health regen. Putting the zone at the start (full health) is a friendly beginner choice — you get the buff immediately.

How to play B. Jenetcontents ↑

Why B. Jenet

B. Jenet (Bonne Jenet) is a graceful, aggressive close-range fighter with superb normals and slippery air movement. She rewards getting in and pressuring — a great pick once you're comfortable moving and blocking, and she has flashy, satisfying combos.

Your first-day game plan

  1. Pick Jenet and walk/hop in
    her normals are excellent — poke with crouch and stand .
  2. Your key move is The Hind
    ↓↘→+ — a knee that launches; it's also a Break move. Your go-to combo ender.
  3. Punish whiffs with Gulf Tomahawk
    ↓↙←+ — an axe kick with good damage.
  4. When you touch them, do one combo
    jump › › ↓↙←+ (Crazy Ivan).

When you're comfortable (intermediate)

  • Crazy Ivan (↓↙←+) is a quick dress swipe — a fine fast special to cancel into.
  • Harrier Bee (air↓+ repeatedly in the air) rains kicks — use it at the peak of a jump for air pressure; mistimed, it gets countered.
  • Skip Buffrass (↓↘→+, a slow tornado projectile) until you understand spacing — the guides warn it's easily jumped.
  • Hit-confirm into super: jump › › Many Many Torpedoes ↓↘→↓↘→+.

Advanced direction

  • Corner BnB: jump / › (2 hits) › The Hind ↓↘→+ › brake › Aurora ↓↘→↓↘→+.
  • Feint combos: stand › Aurora feint ↓+ › crouch › special.
  • T.O.P. Rolling Thunder ( when T.O.P. in) — fast, comboable, and a strong anti-air.
  • Ab Oi Mademoiselle: tapped quickly during Just Defense — a guard-cancel super to blow up someone pressuring you.

Learning path — what to practicecontents ↑

Stage 1 — survive & do one combo

  • Block by holding ← / ↙; stop mashing when it's not your turn.
  • Do the Beginner bread-and-butter 10× in training.
  • Learn your key special and use it on reaction (Terry: Power Dunk anti-air · Jenet: The Hind).

Stage 2 — Garou systems

  • Practice Just Defense (tap ← at the last second) — even once a round is progress.
  • Use hops to approach; add a jump-in to your combo.
  • Hit-confirm into a super when you have meter.
  • Place your T.O.P. zone and feel the damage/regen buff.

Stage 3 — pressure & conversions

  • Land a feint / brake combo.
  • Add the T.O.P. attack () to a combo.
  • Guard-Cancel out of Just Defense into your bread-and-butter.
  • Then chase the Advanced combos.
