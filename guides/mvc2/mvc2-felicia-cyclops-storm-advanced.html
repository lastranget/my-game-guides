MvC2 has four attack buttons — two punches (LP/HP), two kicks (LK/HK) — plus two assists. There is no medium button; old FAQ "strong/forward" attacks are reached by tapping a light twice in a chain.

Strengths & weaknessescontents ↑

Know each character’s job — it explains the point → glue → anchor structure.

Felicia · Rushdown point

Strengths

  • Very fast with huge combo potential.
  • Insane OTG game — Sliding Kick & cr.HK relaunch into more.
  • All three Hypers combo easily; great mobility (run, super-jump, Delta-Kick air control).

Weaknesses

  • Physically weak — low damage output.
  • Frail — takes damage poorly.
  • Short-range normals (a point-blank character); no real zoning; good rollers beat her OTGs.

Cyclops · Assist anchor

Strengths

  • Two of the best beam supers in the game; high-priority ground chains.
  • Great air priority + a double jump; the Gene Splice assist is elite — the team’s glue.
  • Balanced and durable.

Weaknesses

  • No reliable on-point anti-air (Gene Splice is punished on block).
  • No truly safe reversal; middling speed — wins on reads.

Storm · Anchor / runaway

Strengths

  • 2nd-fastest; top-tier rushdown and runaway.
  • 8-way air-dash, flight, tri-jump cross-ups; tons of range.
  • Hail Storm = the safest DHC in the game; Lightning Storm can take 40–70%.
  • The best battery in the game.

Weaknesses

  • Hard to learn and frail; weak chip.
  • Only runaway, not true keep-away; her specials are slow to start/recover.
  • Hail Storm is telegraphed on its own; she’d like better anti-airs.

Assist reference (α / β / γ)contents ↑

The team runs Felicia α · Cyclops β · Storm α — Gene Splice is the key pick that covers the point.

Felicia

α AlphaDelta Kick (DP+) — full-screen, crosses up, knocks down for an OTG. Her best assist.team pick
β BetaSand Splash (QCF+) — fast, safe, low projectile.
γ GammaNeko Spike (DP+) — little reason to pick it.

Cyclops

α AlphaOptic Blast (QCF+) — fast beam; beats fireballs.
β BetaGene Splice (DP+) — elite anti-air; makes Felicia’s rushdown safe. The whole reason he’s here.team pick
γ GammaCyclone Kick (QCB+) — least useful.

Storm

α AlphaWhirlwind (QCF+, Horizontal Typhoon) — a solid projectile assist.team pick
β BetaLightning Attack — an air-dash assist.
γ GammaDouble Typhoon — situational.
Storm’s real value is as the anchor: Hail Storm is the safest DHC in the game and she can runaway to close.

Neutral game & spacingcontents ↑

How each character wins the ground before a combo starts.

Felicia · get in

No zoning — you must approach
Get in with the super-jump, a dash, or Rolling Buckler (faster than a dash). Always with the Gene Splice assist out.
Pokes
Sand Splash reaches ~¾ screen; cr.HK is a fast knockdown sweep that starts your OTG game.
Mistake punisher
Hyper Sand Splash covers the whole screen — punish a whiff from afar.

Cyclops · beam control

Beam game
Mix Optic Blast (low/quick) and the HP angled version — don’t throw them mindlessly.
His anti-air problem
Standing HK is his only real AA and its range is short — that’s why he sits behind the Gene Splice assist.
Punish
Jump back and Super Optic Blast a called assist or beam.

Storm · runaway & rush

Runaway
Super-jump, air-dash and rain Horizontal Typhoon / air fierces; Vertical Typhoon keeps them grounded. Her way to stall out a lead.
Rushdown
Tri-jump cross-ups and her fast dash open people up; convert into the air rave.
Battery
She builds meter faster than anyone — air fierces + the air-throw rave bank ~¾ a bar.

Defense & escaping pressurecontents ↑

Advancing Guard (push-block) blocking
Both punches as you block to shove pressure off. Don’t spam it.
Variable CounterBDBD+
Tag a partner in with a counter mid-blockstring — 1 bar. Good for bailing frail Felicia out.
Reversals
Cyclops’ Gene Splice is fast but punished on block. Felicia & Storm lack a great reversal — block, push-block, and DHC out instead.
Air-guard — and its limitB in air
Hold back in the air to block air attacks/projectiles/supers — but you can’t air-block a grounded normal.
Hail Storm bail
DHC into Storm’s Hail Storm (no recovery) to escape a bad spot and bring her in safely.
SnapbackSnapback
QCF+assist (1 bar) forces out a dangerous point character or deletes a low assist.

Meter game & DHC routescontents ↑

Felicia & Cyclops are the battery
Their combos and chip build bars; bank them for Storm’s kill or a guaranteed DHC.
When to spend
Solo super to confirm a stray hit; DHC to kill or to rescue a character safely (into Hail Storm); save 2+ for a DHC kill.

DHC routes

DHC kill
Dancing Flash Felicia › DHC Mega Optic Blast Cyclops › DHC Lightning Storm Storm
Safe relay / rescue
Mega Optic Blast Cyclops › DHC Hail Storm Storm · no recovery

Mind games & resetscontents ↑

OTG resets (Felicia)
After a knockdown, mix the Sliding-Kick / cr.HK relaunch with a meaty Please Help Me! — but bait good rollers, don’t auto-pilot it.
Get-in cross-up (Felicia)
Super-jump and angle a Delta Kick to come down on the far side; call Gene Splice first so they can’t challenge.
Tick throws (Felicia)
cr.LP, walk up, throw (mash for damage) — she’s fast enough to tick all day.
Tri-jump cross-up (Storm)
Storm’s tri-jump makes her jump-ins ambiguous; convert the open guard into the Lightning Storm air combo.
Pattern-breakers (Cyclops)
A surprise Running Tackle (unblockable) or Gene Splice cracks a turtle.

Common mistakes to breakcontents ↑

Sitting back with Felicia
She has no zoning — if you don’t approach, you lose. Use the super-jump / Rolling Buckler + the assist to get in.
Calling Felicia’s assist into danger
She’s frail — a badly-timed assist can lose her before she even fights.
Leaving Cyclops on point vs rushdown
He has no safe anti-air; he’s the glue, not the front line. Keep him in back behind Gene Splice.
Losing Storm early
She’s your comeback (runaway + Hail Storm). Protect the anchor.
Whiffing Help Me! / Hail Storm raw
Both are telegraphed on their own — use them in combos / as DHCs, not as random throws.
Over-OTGing vs good rollers
Mix in air combos and resets, or they’ll just roll out and punish.
Burning meter on solo supers
When a DHC kills or rescues, a single super is a waste.
Approaching without the assist
Calling Gene Splice before you dash in is the entire point of this team.
