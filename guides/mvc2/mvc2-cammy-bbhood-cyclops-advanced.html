Strengths & weaknessescontents ↑

Know what each character is for — it explains why the team is built point → battery → anchor, and which jobs to lean on.

Cammy · Rushdown point

Strengths

  • Fastest in the game — top movement and attack speed.
  • A full-screen ground dash — the best way to get in.
  • Tiny hitbox (smaller than Jill) — many attacks whiff over her.
  • Owns the air: elite priority, anti-airs, Aerial Raves and OTG loops.
  • Vicious mix-ups, cross-ups and tick-throws.

Weaknesses

  • Bad start-up / recovery on every special and super.
  • Almost no chip damage.
  • Takes above-average damage, deals below-average — poor lasting power.
  • No projectile — she loses a pure keep-away war.
  • Mediocre assists & a poor Variable Combination (BB Hood fixes it).

BB Hood · Zoner / battery

Strengths

  • Elite keep-away: high / low / air missiles at many angles with Guile-fast recovery.
  • Cool Hunting does huge damage and huge chip — and it combos.
  • Builds meter fast — the team’s battery.
  • Tiny and slippery; has a double jump and a crawl for movement tricks.

Weaknesses

  • Almost no anti-air of her own.
  • One of the worst launchers in the game — air combos are minor.
  • Stubby normals (very short range).
  • Cool Hunting has long recovery — punishable if it whiffs.
  • Leans hard on assists — weak when cornered with no meter.

Cyclops · Assist anchor

Strengths

  • Two of the best beam supers in the game (Mega / Super Optic Blast) — great priority, huge block damage, usable in the air.
  • High-priority ground chains and a strong beam keep-away.
  • Great mid-air priority (rivals Spider-Man); a double jump for long air combos.
  • Gene Splice assist = one of the best anti-airs in the game — the team’s glue.
  • Balanced and durable — a great anchor.

Weaknesses

  • No reliable on-point anti-air (Gene Splice is punished on block; fierce beam unreliable; s.HK has short vertical range).
  • No truly safe reversal.
  • Middling speed — wins neutral on reads, not raw rushdown.

Assist reference (α / β / γ)contents ↑

The general guide gives the picks; here is why. The team runs Cammy α · BB Hood α · Cyclops β — two anti-airs plus a knockdown projectile cover the ground and the sky from every angle.

Cammy

α AlphaCannon Spike (DP+) — invincible anti-air; the team’s second get-off-me AAA.team pick
β BetaCannon Drill (QCF+) — fast horizontal ground coverage; for lockdown.
γ GammaAxel Spin Knuckle (QCF+) — spinning punch; rarely chosen.
Variable Combination is Spin Drive Smasher (α/β) — weak on its own, but BB Hood’s bullets pin the foe so it actually connects.

BB Hood

α AlphaSmile & Missile ([B]F+) — knockdown projectile; her only worthwhile assist — pins and zones for the point.team pick
β / γAnti-air flame & basket types exist but are rarely used — α is the clear pick.
Variable Combination is Cool Hunting — huge chip even when blocked.

Cyclops

α AlphaOptic Blast (QCF+) — fast beam; beats fireballs (not beams); covers a partner who is weak at range.
β BetaGene Splice (DP+) — elite anti-air; defensive get-off-me; great for escaping corner pressure; doubles as a decoy.team pick
γ GammaCyclone Kick (QCB+) — poke / combo assist; least useful.
All three Variable Combination into Mega Optic Blast — the team’s closer.

Neutral game & spacingcontents ↑

How to actually win the ground before any combo starts — the bridge between the movelist and a game plan.

Cammy · get in

No projectile — you must approach
Use the full-screen ground dash and dash-jump to close, especially onto a beamer recovering from a shot (Cable). There is no sitting back with Cammy.
Best pokes
Fast /; standing is a long, high-priority anti-air that grants a free Aerial Rave on hit.
Anti-air menu
Cannon Spike (invincible), standing , Cannon Revenge, or just launch.
Slip under beams
Cannon Drill and Spin Drive Smasher travel fast and pass under many beams (Magneto’s EM Disruptor, Cable’s gunfire) and over BB Hood’s low missile — great long-range punishes.

Cyclops · beam control

Beam game (with discipline)
Mix Optic Blast (low/quick) and the angled QCF+ version — but do not throw them mindlessly; the recovery is punishable.
His anti-air problem
Standing is his safest AA but its range is short. He has no great anti-air on point — this is why he sits in back behind the Gene Splice assist.
Punish from afar
Jump back and Super Optic Blast to punish a called assist, a beam, or a projectile. Cancel a blocked Gene Splice into it to stay safe.
Up close
Mix high/low kicks, Cyclone Kick, the launcher, his double jump, and the odd surprise Running Tackle. dominates the air.

BB Hood · build the wall

Two-height missile wall
Alternate [B]F+ (high missile) and [B]F+ (low missile), plus [D]U+ (air) — they can’t crouch under and jump over. Walk in behind it.
Stay safe on fierce
Cancel any blocked or whiffed into a missile — its recovery is tiny, so you’re never left open.
Her only anti-air
Cheer & Fire (flame, angles up). In the air, throw airDP+ flames to wall out approaches — but watch for counters.
Chip with the guns
After a called anti-air assist connects, Cool Hunting for big chip even if it doesn’t fully combo.

Defense & escaping pressurecontents ↑

The moves are in the movelist; this is when to use them — how not to die.

Advancing Guard (push-block) while blocking
Shove the attacker away and cut block-stun — your #1 escape from pressure. Don’t spam it; sometimes it just resets them at a worse range for you.
Variable CounterB-DB-D+ blocking
Tag a partner in with a counter-attack mid-blockstring to escape — costs one bar. Great for bailing Cyclops out of corner pressure.
Reversals (wake-up)
Cammy’s Cannon Spike is invincible — her best wake-up. Cyclops’ Gene Splice is fast but punished on block (cancel into Super Optic Blast to be safe). BB Hood has none — block and push out.
Air-guard — and its limitBin air
Hold back in the air to block air attacks, projectiles and supers. But you cannot air-block a grounded normal — so jumping is not a free escape; you’ll get launched.
Tech rollFon landing
Tap toward or away as you hit the ground from a knockdown to roll out and dodge an OTG.
KBA as a panic button
Cammy’s airKiller Bee Assault has invincible start-up — DHC into it to save a partner from an incoming super (it has beaten Sentinel’s rockets and even countered a Cool Hunting).
Counter the counter
End a blocked chain with Cannon Revenge now and then — it snuffs their mashed poke. Against combo-sprites (Strider, Spider-Man, Wolverine), push-block their string, then Cannon Revenge as they dash back in. Don’t repeat it — it’s readable.

Meter game & DHC routescontents ↑

This team banks meter on BB Hood and cashes it on Cyclops. Decide when to spend here; full combo routes live in the combo guide.

BB Hood is the battery
Her missiles and Cool Hunting build bars fast. Bank them — for Cyclops’ kill, or to spend on her own monstrous chip.
When to spend
Solo super to confirm a stray hit. DHC to kill, or to bring a fresh character in safely. Snapback (Snapback, 1 bar) to force out a scary point character or delete a low assist. Save 2+ for a guaranteed DHC kill.
Fix Cammy’s bad Variable Combination
On its own her VC whiffs. With BB Hood behind her, the bullets pin the foe in the corner so every hit of Spin Drive Smasher connects — a win-win even on block (Cool Hunting chip).

Mind games & resetscontents ↑

Opening up a defender once you’ve cornered them.

Tick-throw (Cammy)
A has great range/speed — tick with it, then Suplex (F+), then OTG D+, D+ into an Aerial Rave.
Cross-up + assist
Dash-jump to the far side and call a close-range / dasher assist before you leave the ground. As you land behind them the assist hits — they can’t tell which way to block. Launch the opened guard.
Air Suplex side-switch (Cammy)
Her air throw switches sides (so the wrong tech-roll direction whiffs) and lets you OTG afterward.
Instant super-jump escape (Cammy)
If a launcher is blocked, tap up to instantly super-jump out of the punish — up-forward to cross to the other side (deadly if you also called an assist).
DHC confuser
Whiff or get Spin Drive Smasher blocked on purpose, then DHC — the follow-up super catches them repositioning to punish.
Crawl mix-up (BB Hood)
Stalking crawls under a fireball or super and comes out the other side — bait, then punish.
Pattern-breakers (Cyclops)
A random Running Tackle (unblockable) or surprise Gene Splice cracks a turtle’s rhythm. Vs heavy blockers, BB Hood’s Hyper Apple For You grab super beats guarding outright.

Matchups vs the top tierscontents ↑

MvC2 is defined by a handful of characters. Here is how this team handles them, then a quick primer by play-style.

Magnetotop tier — very hard
The fastest, strongest rushdown in the game — one opening can kill. Lead with the Gene Splice / Cannon Spike assists for cross-up protection, contest his approach with Cammy’s invincible Cannon Spike, and time airKiller Bee Assault to phase through EM Disruptor and punish. Do not get opened.
Stormtop tier — hard
Everyone struggles, but she takes more damage than Cammy. One airKiller Bee Assault punishes a Whirlwind/Typhoon or her runaway fly game (she can’t block while flying). Cannon Revenge her dive-ins; match her air mobility with Cannon Drill/airCannon Strike. Strip her typhoon & fly and her options collapse.
Sentineltop tier — winnable
His stomp-rushdown chips on block. Push-block the stomp or stuff it with Cannon Spike / the Gene Splice assist. He’s huge — he eats ~4 KBA combos, so stay patient and play act/react. Cyclops’ beams contest his drones.
Cabletop tier — patience
He wins a pure keep-away war (Cammy has no projectile). Anticipate the Viper Beam → airKiller Bee Assault (invincible) to dodge and punish; or dash-jump + air Cannon Drill to get behind his weak air-defence. Cammy is short enough to duck his gunfire. Let BB Hood / Cyclops contest the projectile war.
Spiraltop tier — toughest
Considered the hardest character to face. Don’t throw out laggy specials except in combos. airKiller Bee Assault her when she rises to replenish swords; block the sword wall and punish the gaps. Pure reflexes and patience; lean on Cyclops’ beams + assists to contest space.

By archetype

vs Rushdown
The Gene Splice + Cannon Spike assists are your wall. Push-block to reset space, then KBA / super the gap. Don’t mash into their pressure.
vs Keep-away / runaway
Close with Cammy’s dash; slip Cannon Drill/Spin Drive Smasher under beams; BB Hood can out-zone some keepaway outright.
vs Turtle
Cool Hunting chip + Hyper Apple For You (unblockable grab) + Cyclops beam chip pry them open. Snapback their anchor to force a fight.

Common mistakes to breakcontents ↑

Habits that quietly lose games — fix these first.

Throwing beams / missiles mindlessly
Good players punish the recovery. Mix heights and cancel projectiles into specials.
Forgetting Cammy has no projectile
You must approach — don’t sit back and lose the keep-away war you can’t win.
Jumping to escape pressure
You can’t air-block a grounded normal — you’ll just get launched. Block or push-block instead.
Over-chaining lights
Damage scaling guts your finisher. Keep chains short — is plenty.
Repeating reversals / counters
Cannon Spike, Gene Splice and Cannon Revenge are all readable. Bait one out, then punish.
Burning meter on solo supers
When a DHC would kill or rescue, a single super is a waste. Plan the bars.
Leaving Cyclops on point vs rushdown
He has no safe anti-air — he’s the anchor for a reason. Keep him in back behind Gene Splice.
Approaching without calling an assist
Calling Gene Splice / Cannon Spike before you dash in is the entire point of this team.
Cornering yourself as BB Hood with no meter
Her launcher and anti-air are weak — she needs space and bars to function.

Beating Arcade mode — Abysscontents ↑

The final boss has three forms. Zone the first, out-manoeuvre the second, nuke the third.

Form 1 — armoured humanoid
Just combo him to death — e.g. Cyclops XX Cyclone Kick. When he fires the big cannon, make sure you’re already on the safe side, then go back to beams/missiles.
Form 2 — orange blob
Zone it down with Optic Blast and missiles. When the bubble projectiles come out, super-jump then double-jump over them and keep chipping.
Form 3 — small humanoid
Open with a jumping Mega Optic Blast, or simply DHC / THC him down — big beams melt this last form fast.
