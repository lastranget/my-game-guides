Cammy / Morrigan / Cyclops — a beginner's team guide

A beginner-friendly guide to the Cammy / Morrigan / Cyclops team in Marvel vs. Capcom 2 — built for someone new to fighting games and new to MvC2. Start at the top, learn one thing at a time, and use the per-character moveset cards (each sized to fit one screen of your AYN Thor). Inputs are shown as coloured buttons that match your Thor face buttons.

MvC2 has only four attack buttons — two punches (LP/HP) and two kicks (LK/HK) — plus two assist buttons (A1/A2). There is no medium attack like in Street Fighter.

Motions are written relative to your facing — F = toward the opponent, B = away — so they read correctly on both sides of the screen.

Directions are written as arrows from the character's point of view (facing right): ↓↘→ means roll the stick from down to forward. A move like Spiral Arrow = QCF+ means "quarter-circle forward + a kick". Combos read left→right; the › just separates the steps.

The complete command list — every move & team mechanic

These work for every character. Directions are shown for a character facing right; flip left/right when you switch sides. bar = how many levels of the Hyper Combo meter it spends.

Movement
Dash / runFFor
Tap toward the foe twice (or press both punches) to dash forward; back-back (or hold ← + PP) dashes away. Big, slow characters live and die on this.
Super jumpD+U
Flick from any down position straight to up (↓→↑) to leap far higher than a normal jump — this is how you chase a launched opponent for an air combo.
Air dash / flightcharacter-specific
Not universal, but several characters can move in the air. Morrigan can air-dash (→→ / ←← in the air) and fly — see her moveset. Most others just have one normal jump and one super jump.
Defense
Block / air guardBorDB
Hold away to block: standing ← stops highs/overheads, crouch ↙ stops lows. You can block in the air — hold ← while airborne to air-guard air attacks, projectiles and supers. (You can’t air-block a grounded foe’s ground normals, so jumping isn’t a free escape.)
Advancing Guard (push-block) while blocking
Press both punches the instant you block to shove the attacker away (blue flash). Cuts block-stun and buys space — your #1 tool for escaping pressure.
ThrowB/F+or
Up close, press toward/away + HP (or HK) to grab. Beats opponents who block too much. Out of range it whiffs and is punishable.
Air throwU+or
In the air next to an airborne foe, up + HP/HK slams them down — a strong anti-air against jumpers.
Down recovery (tech roll)B-DB-D+or
As you hit the ground after a knockdown, roll back→down + any attack to recover instantly instead of lying there — dodges the opponent’s wake-up offense.
Offense
Chain combo (Magic Series)
Every character can chain normals weak→strong (e.g. LP › LK › HP › HK). Each automatically cancels into the next — the backbone of all bread-and-butter combos.
Launcher → Aerial RaveDF+or
↘ (down-forward) + HP or HK pops the foe up (red flash). Hold ↑ to super-jump after them and air-chain for an “Aerial Rave”. (Exact launch button varies per character — see the movesets.)
Special moveQCF+
Free moves done with a stick motion + attack — fireballs, uppercuts, charge moves. Cost no meter. Each character’s specials are listed in the movesets.
Hyper Combo (super)1 barQCF+or
Your big-damage move: usually a special’s motion + two punches or two kicks. The screen darkens briefly on start-up. Spends one meter level (a few cost two).
Hyper Combo gaugebottom bar · max 5
The meter at the bottom. It fills as you attack, get hit, or block; you can bank up to 5 levels. Supers, counters, snapbacks and team supers all spend from it.
Team play (the heart of MvC2)
Variable Assist (call a partner)freeor
Tap A1 or A2 to call that teammate in for one set attack, then they leave. The called partner can be hit while on screen, but that damage is recoverable (red). This is the engine of the whole team.
Variable Attack (tag / switch)free+or+
LP+LK swaps to partner A, HP+HK to partner B — the incoming partner flies in with an attack as the old one leaves. If the attack hits, the foe is launched and you can follow up.
Variable Counter (alpha counter)1 barB-DB-D+or
While blocking, roll back→down + A1/A2 to call that partner in to counter-attack and steal your turn back out of block-stun.
Variable Combination (team super)1–3 bars+
Press both assist buttons together for a team super: one character per meter level you have (up to all three) fires a Hyper Combo at once. Massive damage and a great panic / get-off-me button. Must be grounded and not already attacking.
Snapback1 barQCF+or
A quick super-flash hit; if it lands it knocks the active enemy out and forces in their partner (A1 = their first partner, A2 = their second). Use it to kill a fragile assist character or drag out someone you beat. Costs a bar whether or not it connects.
Delayed Hyper Combo (DHC)1 bar eachduring a super → a partner’s super command
While your character’s super is happening, input another character’s super command (e.g. QCF+PP) to instantly cancel into theirs. Chain up to all three supers back-to-back for huge damage — or to bail out of a super that would otherwise be punished.
Character Order Changefreeor between battles
On the between-battle screen, hold A1 or A2 to make that teammate your point (lead) character for the next round.
TauntStart
Press Start to taunt — pure style, no mechanical effect (a couple of characters aside).

Six things every fighting-game beginner needs

  1. Block by holding away. Hold ← away from the opponent to block high/standing hits; hold ↙ (down-back) to block lows. You can block in the air too — hold ← while airborne to air-guard air attacks, projectiles and supers (you can’t air-block a grounded foe’s ground normals, so jumping isn’t a free escape).
  2. Chain your normals (the "magic series"). Press attacks weak→strong: . Each one cancels into the next automatically — this is the backbone of every combo.
  3. Launch & fly. A launcher (usually D+ or ) knocks them up; tap ↑ to super-jump after them, then do an air chain . That's an "air combo".
  4. Specials & supers cost nothing / cost meter. Specials (fireballs, uppercuts) are free. Supers use a stock of the meter bar at the bottom — they're your big damage and chip.
  5. Assists are your other two characters. Tap or to call a teammate for one attack. This team is built around calling Cyclops to cover you.
  6. Throw when they block too much. Walk up and press + (or a direction + the throw buttons) to grab a turtling opponent.

How to play this teamcontents ↑

Why this team (in plain terms)

Cammy gets in your opponent's face and does damage. Morrigan throws fireballs, builds meter, and is a comfortable backup. Cyclops sits in the back, builds the comeback, and — most importantly — his assist is one of the best "get-off-me" anti-airs in the game, which makes Cammy's offense safe.

Team order: Cammy → Morrigan → Cyclops. Assists to pick: Cyclops β (Gene Splice), Morrigan β (Soul Fist), Cammy α (Cannon Spike).

Your first-day game plan (keep it this simple)

  1. Pick Cammy. Walk forward. When you touch the opponent, do › QCF+. That single combo is a totally fine way to start winning.
  2. Call Cyclops () before you jump in or dash in. His uppercut covers you if they try to jump or mash a button.
  3. Block when it's not your turn. Hold ← / ↙. Patience beats most beginners.
  4. When you have a full meter bar, end a combo with a super instead: › QCF+.

When you're comfortable (intermediate)

  • Open people up. Mix high (jump-in ), low (crouching ), and throw. Always call the Cyclops assist first so they can't just jump out.
  • Approach behind a fireball. Call Morrigan Soul Fist () and dash in right behind it with Cammy — the fireball is a moving wall.
  • Learn one air combo per character (see the Intermediate combos). Launch → super-jump → chain → special/super.
  • Bank meter for Cyclops. The front two build it; spend it on Mega Optic Blast for chip and DHCs.

Advanced direction (where to grow)

  • DHC (Delayed Hyper Combo): super-cancel between characters. The team ender is → Cyclops Mega Optic Blast (QCF+). E.g. Morrigan Silhouette Blade › DHC › Cyclops Mega Optic Blast.
  • OTG extensions: Cammy's Spiral Arrow and Optic Sweep hit downed opponents — use them to tack supers onto knockdowns (see Advanced combos).
  • Assist extensions: call an assist mid-combo to keep the opponent in place and add hits.
  • Snapbacks & safe DHCs: later, learn to force in a specific opponent character and to DHC out of danger. Save these for after the fundamentals feel automatic.

If Cammy dies: fall back to Morrigan zoning (air Soul Fists, Shadow Blade anti-air), feed meter to Cyclops, and use Mega Optic Blast chip to close.

Learning path — what to practicecontents ↑

Stage 1 — Survive & do one combo

  • Hold ← / ↙ to block; learn to stop pressing buttons when it isn't your turn.
  • Do each character's Beginner bread-and-butter 10 times in training mode.
  • Call the Cyclops assist before approaching — make it a habit.

Stage 2 — Convert & pressure

  • Land a launch → air combo (Intermediate) consistently with Cammy.
  • Mix one high, one low, one throw. Always with an assist call.
  • Approach behind a Morrigan Soul Fist.
  • End combos with a super when you have meter.

Stage 3 — Team play

  • Do one DHC into Cyclops Mega Optic Blast.
  • Add one OTG super after a knockdown.
  • Learn to run away & build meter with Morrigan when you're ahead.
  • Then chase the Advanced combos and assist extensions.
